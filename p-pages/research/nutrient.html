Mitigating the Nutrient Collapse

Reduced CO2 microclimate generation via Chlorella algae spatial dominance in a resilient permaculture system for nutritionally desirable food crops

The nutrient collapse is a relatively unknown impact of climate change. Elevated atmospheric CO2 can reduce the nutritional profile of numerous staple food crops. For this speculative project I conducted in-depth qualitative and quantitative research across a range of disciplines involving resilience, ecosystem health, microclimates, vernacular landscape design, agroforestry, permaculture, crop biology and more. Both methodical and creative amalgamation of this research into a cohesive whole led to a speculative permaculture design intervention to reduced hyper-localised atmospheric CO2 concentrations.

The specific combination, layout and construction of the space generates a localised microclimate of reduced atmospheric CO2 as a result of the vegeation, food crops and landscape design. Specifically, the properties of Chlorella algae as a potent CO2 sequester is the primary CO2 reduction vehicle. This process is supported by a range of other processes and agents that support each other should any of them collapse – generating resilence in uncertain environments. The reduced atmospheric CO2 microclimate generated over the agricultural space enables food crops to grow with a more desirable nutritional profile, thus eliminating the potential nutritional deficiencies.
